What is HR compliance?

HR compliance means adhering to all applicable laws and regulations that impact the rights and protections of workers. These laws can differ between countries, states, provinces, and territories and impact employees and contractors.Â 

The primary employment laws present throughout the world that impact employees include the following:

  • Minimum wage

  • Overtime pay

  • Work hours

  • Rest breaks

  • Employment agreements

  • Employee benefit entitlements such as PTO, parental leave, and pensions

  • Probationary periods

  • Background checks

  • Payment methods

  • Payment schedules

  • Payroll taxes

  • Payroll records

  • Termination

The primary laws and regulations impacting independent contractors are significantly different from those affecting employees since contractors are self-employed, which means they control their work schedule, pay their own taxes, and arenâ€™t entitled to statutory benefits by law.

Laws for contractors include the following:

  • Right to a contractor agreement
  • Right to control their work
  • Right to intellectual property
  • Right to work when and where they want
  • Right to market their servicesÂ 
  • Right to negotiate payment and choose the payment method
  • Right to work with other clients and contractors
  • Right to challenge their work classification
  • Right to file their own taxes and claim deductionsÂ 

Non-compliance with these laws creates compliance risk. Compliance management is essential to prevent court cases, fines, loss of talent, and reputation damage for your startup.
