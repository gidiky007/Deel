In todays competitive talent landscape, enterprise companies face numerous challenges when it comes to attracting and retaining top talent. Global skills shortages, increased global competition, and changing worker preferences are all putting pressure on businesses to rethink their approach to talent acquisition and retention.

One way that companies can address these challenges is by offering location flexibility to their employees. Location flexibility gives employees the freedom to work from anywhere in the world, supporting work-life balance and leading to happier and more productive employees likely to stay with the company longer.

Lets explore the benefits of location flexibility for enterprise businesses in more detail.

What are the benefits of location flexibility for enterprise businesses?

Retain talent

One of the benefits of offering location flexibility to employees is that it can help to improve employee retention. The pandemic changed how people think about work, causing many to prioritize well-being and work-life balance. This change led to the great resignation, whereby many workers quit their jobs in pursuit of employment that allowed greater control over their time and work and would not be as stressful as their previous jobs.

35% of employees who left their jobs during the pandemic said they were looking for a new job that offered more flexibility, according to Qualtrics research. And 81% of employees said they would be more loyal to their employers if they had flexible work options, according to a 2020 survey by FlexJobs. This is because location flexibility gives employees more control over their work, which can lead to increased satisfaction, motivation, and employee engagement.

Location flexibility can also support personal career development, giving current employees the incentive to explore different facets of an organization and experience working with new market sectors or divisions within the company.

Finally, a business with location flexibility enables employees to continue working even if the main office is closed due to a natural disaster or other disruptive event. This flexibility can help to protect the businesss resilience and ensure that it can continue to operate even during difficult times.

When the conflict started in Europe, some of our team members in Ukraine had to relocate. Thanks to Deel, we were able to provide those team members with job security and flexibility, which helped reduce the amount of stress they were feeling at the time.

Carmen Fan,

Head of Marketing

Attract talent

Location flexibility is also one of the most effective talent acquisition strategies, helping hiring managers attract potential candidates. A study by the UK government found that job adverts offering flexible working attracted 30% more applicants. In addition, 51% of job seekers would forgo higher salaries for more flexibility.

Location flexibility also empowers businesses to attract talented job seekers from a global talent pool. Companies gain a competitive edge in the global labor market by building an employer brand that makes it clear that the company is open to remote workers and provides flexible work options.

Reduce costs

Offering location flexibility can also help businesses reduce the overhead costs of accommodating on-site workers. Companies can save on rent, utilities, and office supplies. Additionally, businesses can save on travel expenses by allowing employees to attend meetings and conferences virtually.

What are the challenges of location flexibility for enterprise businesses?

Of course, there are also some challenges associated with offering location flexibility. For example, companies must ensure employees have the right tools and resources to be productive when working remotely. They must also ensure that their communication and collaboration systems are up to the task.

How to implement location flexibility in enterprise organizations

There are several steps that enterprise organizations can take to implement location flexibility. These steps include the following:

Step 1: Build internal infrastructure: Businesses need to adopt best practices to ensure they can continue functioning effectively with geographically distributed workers. This includes embracing asynchronous working, investing in digital communication and collaboration tools, and setting clear performance metrics. Learn more with our ultimate guide to remote teams.

Step 2: Embrace a flexible workplace culture: Location flexibility will only be effective if the company embodies, promotes, and supports a flexible work environment. Management must lead by example and create a company culture where employees feel comfortable and encouraged to work from anywhere.

Step 3: Create supporting policies and guidelines: Employees need clear, flexible work policies and guidelines that outline the expectations for remote workers. This includes things like hours of work, communication expectations, and how to handle travel logistics and expenses.
